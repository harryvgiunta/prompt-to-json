The goals of this policy are to:

- make it clear when software, contracts, docs, or services are official;

- prevent confusing forks, marketplace packs, services, or integrations from implying endorsement; and
- keep the name useful for the community.

## Allowed uses

You may use the prompt-to-json name without separate permission when you are:

- referring to the official project truthfully, such as "compatible with prompt-to-json";
- linking to the official project;
- describing that your software uses, integrates with, or is based on prompt-to-json;
- preserving notices required by the project licenses;
- using the name in package metadata, docs, blog posts, videos, talks, or tutorials in a way that is not misleading; or
- making non-commercial community references that do not imply official status.

Examples of acceptable wording:

- "Works with prompt-to-json."
- "A contract pack for prompt-to-json."
- "Forked from prompt-to-json."
- "Powered by prompt-to-json."

You may fork and modify the code under its copyright license, but if you distribute a modified version you must avoid confusing users about whether it is the official project.

Recommended naming examples:

- "Acme JSON Contracts, based on prompt-to-json"
- "Acme fork of prompt-to-json"

Avoid names that imply the fork is the official project or successor unless you have written permission.

## Contract packs and marketplace packs

Official starter contracts distributed by this repository may refer to prompt-to-json as part of the official project.

Third-party or marketplace contract packs may say they are "for prompt-to-json" or "compatible with prompt-to-json", but they must not imply they are official, certified, endorsed, or maintained by Harry Giunta unless they have written permission.

Third-party packs should use their own distinctive pack names and should include their own license file or license metadata.

If project logos or visual assets are published, do not use them as the primary branding for your own product, service, pack, website, or company without permission. Do not modify them in a way that implies official endorsement.
